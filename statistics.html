How Our Projections Are Calculated

Lifetime Defaults

There are three factors required as an input to calculate the projected loss curve. These factors include the portfolio mix (weighting of loans by loan grade and term), the expected default rate for each loan grade and the slope of the curve which indicates the time-based distribution of loan defaults.

3) Distribution of Defaults

The distribution of defaults is calculated using the exponential distribution function as explained by the following function:
F(x; λ) = 1 - e-λx.

For this function, we use a value ranging from 0.05 to 0.6 depending on the Loan Grade and Loan Term for lambda [λ] (where x is time from loan start). We generated these projections by looking at the λ seen on projected and actual default curves of comparable international p2p lending platforms.
